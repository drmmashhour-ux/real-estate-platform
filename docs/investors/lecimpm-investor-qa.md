## 1. Product

**Q: What is LECIPM?**

**A:** LECIPM is an **AI-powered operating system for real estate** — not just listings. It **captures and attributes leads**, runs **deal intelligence**, **guides brokers** with assistive tooling (including listing and workflow assistants), and uses **feedback loops from outcomes** to improve scoring and marketplace signals. Humans stay accountable; AI **supports** execution.

---

**Q: How is this different from platforms like Centris?**

**A:** **Centris** is fundamentally about **listing distribution** and syndication reach. **LECIPM** sits on the **operations and intelligence layer**: conversion, CRM, deal file, compliance-oriented surfacing, and optimization signals. We **complement** syndicated inventory — we do not replace regulatory listing systems — by improving **what happens after the listing exists**: leads, pipeline, deals, and broker efficiency.

---

**Q: Who is the customer?**

**A:** Primary **brokerages and brokers** using the CRM and deal workflows; adjacent users include **sellers/buyers** on public journeys and, where enabled, **investors** consuming analytics-style surfaces. Packaging may vary by tier.

## 2. Market

**Q: Why now?**

**A:** Residential platforms have largely **stopped at discovery and listings**. Cheap **compute and structured data** finally allow **measurable optimization** of funnel, pricing posture, and broker execution — without pretending to replace licensed judgment. Regulatory pressure (privacy, disclosures) also rewards **systems that bake compliance workflows in**, not bolt-on spreadsheets.

## 5. Defensibility

**Q: What is your moat?**

**A:**

- **Workflow + data integration** — leads, deals, listings, and events in one system (network effects over time)  
- **Self-improving signals** — outcome-backed patterns and bounded score updates (**not black-box autonomy**)  
- **Deep broker workflow embedding** — daily CRM use vs. occasional search  
- **Compliance and insurance-aligned surfaces** — differentiated in sensitive categories (e.g. Québec co-ownership context)  

Moat **compounds with usage density**, not with a single model release.

## 6. Risk

**Q: Regulatory risks?**

**A:** We design for **Québec**: **Law 25** (privacy/consent/minimization), **OACIQ** obligations (agency, disclosures, supervision). AI is framed as **assistive** — it does **not** replace mandated brokerage duties or legal advice. Features are gated so **auto-outreach to consumers** is avoided where policy requires human initiation.

---

**Q: Dependency on Centris?**

**A:** **No unhealthy dependency.** Syndicated feeds are **partners**, not the core IP. LECIPM adds **conversion and intelligence** on top; we **respect partner rules** — **no unauthorized Centris data usage**. If integration scope changes, the product still stands on **native leads, CRM, and deals**.

---

**Q: AI / reputational risk?**

**A:** We emphasize **deterministic or auditable surfaces**, **broker review gates**, and **clear disclaimers** on ROI or ranking outputs. Escalation paths for bad outputs are **product + policy**, not “move fast and automate messaging.”

---

## 7. Scaling

**Q: How do you scale?**

**A:**

- **Broker acquisition** — territory-by-territory depth before horizontal expansion  
- **Lead funnel** — partnerships, content, and controlled paid demand  
- **Product leverage** — AI assistants, intelligence, and marketplace optimizer outputs improve with usage (**data efficiency**, not headcount-linear scaling)  

---

**Q: International expansion?**

**A:** Near-term **depth in core geography**; expansion requires **localized compliance** (privacy, advertising, brokerage rules). Architecture is modular; **go-to-market** must follow regulation, not the reverse.

---

**Q: Long-term vision?**

**A:** To become a **trusted intelligence and execution layer** for real estate transactions — where **every serious deal** benefits from structured data, assistive AI, and measurable improvement — **without** replacing the licensed professional as the accountable party.
