# Slide 2 — Problem

## Why the market is broken

| Problem | Why it hurts |
|--------|----------------|
| **Fragmented real estate platforms** | Buyers, renters, and brokers use multiple tools; no single place for discovery and transactions. |
| **Inefficient broker workflows** | CRM, listings, and client communication are disconnected; deal and lead intelligence is scattered. |
| **No integrated property investment discovery** | Deals and off-market opportunities are hard to find; analytics and ROI visibility are limited. |
| **Trust and fraud in short-term rentals** | Safety and verification vary by platform; guests and hosts lack consistent protection. |
| **No intelligent analytics for property decisions** | Pricing, demand, and risk are not unified—leading to poor decisions and operational cost. |

**Bottom line:** Current tools are point solutions. No one has built a **trusted, integrated ecosystem** that serves owners, professionals, travelers, and investors in one place.

# Slide 3 — Solution

## One platform, full ecosystem

LECIPM integrates everything in **one trusted environment**:

| Pillar | Role |
|--------|------|
| **Real estate marketplace** | Listings for sale and long-term rental; verified and searchable. |
| **Short-term rental marketplace (BNHub)** | Full stay lifecycle: list, book, pay, review—with host and guest tools. |
| **Broker tools** | CRM, leads, listings, and pipeline in the same platform as the marketplace. |
| **Owner tools** | Dashboards for listings, bookings, revenue, and communication. |
| **Investment intelligence** | Deals, analytics, and ROI visibility for investors and professionals. |
| **Trust & safety infrastructure** | Verification, fraud detection, disputes, and enforcement from day one. |
| **AI automation** | Pricing, fraud, moderation, and recommendations to scale operations. |

**Why it works:** One identity, one data layer, one governance model—so supply, demand, and professionals grow together instead of in silos.

# Slide 6 — Business Model

## How we make money

| Revenue stream | Mechanism |
|----------------|-----------|
| **Booking commissions (BNHub)** | Guest service fee + host commission on each short-term booking—scales with volume. |
| **Real estate commissions** | Percentage of sale or long-term lease value when transactions are originated or closed on the platform. |
| **Subscription plans** | Broker CRM and Owner dashboard tiers (Basic → Professional → Agency/Enterprise)—recurring revenue. |
| **Promoted listings** | Paid visibility in search and discovery for listings and deals. |
| **Analytics subscriptions** | Premium investment and market analytics for investors and professionals. |
| **Referral programs** | Incentives for referring hosts, guests, and brokers—with abuse controls to protect unit economics. |

**Scalability:** Revenue grows with **transaction volume** (commissions), **subscription adoption** (brokers and owners), and **promotion and referrals**. Model is transparent and localizable by market.

**Why it’s defensible:** Integration depth (one ecosystem, one data layer, one governance model) and built-in trust and AI make it hard to replicate with point solutions.

# Slide 9 — Roadmap

## Development milestones

| Phase | Focus |
|-------|--------|
| **Phase 1 — Platform foundation** | Infrastructure, auth, users, database, and core APIs. |

| **Phase 7 — Multi-market expansion** | Pilot launch (e.g. Montreal); then regional and international expansion with local compliance. |

**Outcome:** A working pilot (list, search, book, pay, trust systems) before scaling dashboards, AI, and new markets.
