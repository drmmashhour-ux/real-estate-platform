## Product

### Q: What exactly are you building?

We’re building **LECIPM** as a **real-estate ecosystem platform** — listings, professional workflows, and the surrounding operations layer — and **BNHub** as the **short-term rental** product: discovery, booking, and host tools **connected** to that ecosystem. The product thesis is **one backbone** for property and stays, not two disconnected stacks. What’s **shipped vs pilot vs roadmap** should match your deck — say it plainly.

### Q: How is BNHub different from existing platforms?

We’re **not** claiming to out-feature global OTAs on day one. BNHub is aimed at **integration with real-estate operations** and **clearer host and guest flows** where we’ve built them — fees, verification, admin — so **operators and multi-property hosts** aren’t duct-taping five tools together. Differentiation is **depth in our segment**, not “we beat Airbnb at everything.”

### Q: Why combine real estate and short-term rentals?

Because in the real world the **same owners, managers, and agencies** touch **long-term and short-term** inventory. When those worlds sit in separate products, **data and trust don’t carry over** — duplicate entry, inconsistent policies, slower support. Combining them lets us improve **transparency and efficiency** for the businesses that actually run both.

### Q: Who is the customer?

**Guests and hosts** use BNHub for stays; **brokers, managers, and property businesses** use LECIPM for the broader real-estate surface. In early stages we often **narrow** to one wedge — e.g. a city, a partner type, or a host segment — and say that explicitly.

### Q: What is your roadmap?

**Near term:** solidify core flows you’ve committed to — search, listing, booking, host ops — and whatever LECIPM modules are **live**. **Next:** density in priority markets, partnerships, and trust/compliance depth. Share **milestones**, not a ten-year feature laundry list.

## Market

### Q: How big is the market?

**Short-term rentals** and **real-estate / proptech software** are **large, established categories** — directional TAM is easy to cite from public sources. Our plan is **bottom-up**: cities or segments where we can prove **repeat bookings and partner pull**, not “we capture X% of global TAM.” Forward share is **assumption**, not fact.

### Q: Why now?

**Behavior** already shifted: guests expect instant booking and clear pricing; operators expect software, not spreadsheets. **Regulation and trust** expectations are higher, which rewards **transparent workflows**. **Why us / why this team** is separate — answer with your specific insight, not “the market is hot.”

### Q: Which market are you starting with?

We start where we can get **density**: a **defined geography**, **partner type**, or **host segment** — name yours. The goal is **liquidity and operational proof** before we spray into new regions. If you’re still choosing, say you’re **testing two hypotheses** and what metric will decide.

### Q: Is this a winner-take-all market?

**Consumer discovery** skews to strong brands. **B2B operations and integrated supply** can support **several** serious players. We’re building for **segments that value integrated ops**, not assuming we replace global habit overnight.

## Competition

### Q: How do you compete with Airbnb?

We **respect** their scale and consumer habit. We don’t win by claiming **generic** “better UX for everyone.” We compete where **integrated real-estate + stays**, **partner-led supply**, or **local depth** matter — and we earn **step by step**. If they improve a feature we have, we still win on **focus and integration** in our wedge — if we execute.

### Q: What is your advantage?

**Structural:** one platform for **property + short-term** workflows. **Operational:** faster iteration and **closer relationships** with a narrow customer set. **Honest version:** advantage is **only real if** we ship trust and ops depth — we describe **what’s live** and **what we’re proving next**.

### Q: Why would users switch?

**Guests** switch when **clarity, support, or inventory** is better for the trip they want — often **niche supply** or **direct host** relationships. **Hosts** switch when **economics, control, or tooling** beats their current stack — especially **multi-property** operators. We don’t assume mass switching; we **earn** a slice with a **clear wedge**.

### Q: Who else is in the space?

OTAs, channel managers, PMS tools, local listing sites — the landscape is **crowded**. We map **2×2** by **consumer vs pro** and **stays-only vs property ecosystem** and show where we sit. Moat language stays **modest**: integration, data with permission, workflow depth — not “no one can copy us.”

## Traction

### Q: How many users do you have?

**Answer with the truth.** Example patterns: “We have **[N] registered users / active in last 30 days**” or “We’re **pre-launch**; we have **[N] waitlist / pilot conversations**.” If the number is small, pair it with **what you learned** — don’t apologize; **reframe** as validation stage.

### Q: What validation do you have?

List **evidence types** you actually have: **pilot hosts**, **LOIs**, **paid tests**, **repeat usage**, **partner intros**, **product demos completed**, **NPS from interviews**. Avoid blending **“interest”** with **“usage.”** If early: “We’re validating **[hypothesis]** with **[method]**; so far **[signal]**.”

### Q: What have you learned so far?

Use **specific** learnings: e.g. **which listing type converts**, **what blocks booking**, **what ops integration matters**. One **concrete** learning beats three vague positives. If you haven’t learned much yet, say you’re **running structured tests** and what you’ll know in **30–60 days**.

### Q: What are your key metrics?

Name **3–5** you actually track: e.g. **listings**, **bookings / GMV**, **activation**, **retention**, **pipeline**. For anything **estimated**, say “**target / assumption** — we’ll validate with this round.” Never **smooth** pilots into “MRR” without qualification.

## Business model

### Q: How do you make money?

**Take rate** on bookings **when** transactions run on-platform. **Premium** host or pro **subscriptions / features**. **Featured placement** or **lead products** where ROI is clear for the supplier. Point to **[financials.md](financials.md)** for **labeled estimates** — not audited P&L.

### Q: What are your margins?

**Marketplace** gross margin depends on **payment processing**, **support load**, and **disputes** — early stage is **guesswork**. Say: “We model **take rate minus direct variable cost**; **net margin** comes after we have **volume** — figures in our appendix are **assumptions**.” If you have **early unit economics**, cite **one** honest datapoint.

### Q: When do you become profitable?

**Honest early-stage answer:** profitability is a **function of scale and mix** — we’re focused on **contribution margin per booking / per city** first. Give a **range** only if it’s in your model and **labeled as illustrative**. Avoid a fake precise year unless you can defend it.

### Q: How do you price vs competitors?

We **anchor** on **value** — time saved, **yield**, **control** — not a race to the bottom on fees. If we’re **lower** take rate early, say **why** (density building) and **when** you’d normalize — as **strategy**, not promise.

## Growth

### Q: How will you acquire users?

**Layered:** **content** and **SEO** where relevant, **direct outreach** to hosts and partners, **partnerships** with agencies and operators who **already** have supply. We **measure** cost per qualified lead and per **activated listing** — and **double down** on what works.

### Q: What is your CAC?

If **measured:** give **definition** (e.g. CAC to **activated host**) and **period**. If **not yet stable:** “We’re **running channel tests**; early read is **[directional only]** — I’ll share **solid** CAC once we have **[N]** cohorts.” **Do not** invent a CAC.

### Q: What channels work best?

**Honest:** “**So far, [channel]** has driven **[signal]**; we’re still testing.” If nothing works yet: “We’re **hypothesis-testing** three channels with **[budget and success metric]**.” Investors prefer **process** over fake certainty.

### Q: What is your go-to-market motion?

**Bottom-up** host acquisition, **top-down** partner deals, or **hybrid** — pick what you **actually** do. Tie to **one city or segment** first. Reference **[enterprise-sales.md](enterprise-sales.md)** if you use a B2B motion.

## Technology

### Q: What is your tech stack?

We run a **TypeScript** **monorepo** — **web apps** (e.g. Next.js-style stack where applicable), **APIs**, **shared packages**, and **services** (e.g. auth, AI) as the product grows. **Data** is managed with **Prisma** and a **relational database** in our main app path. **Mobile** may be **roadmap or beta** — state what’s **true** in your build.

### Q: Why is it scalable?

**Horizontal scaling** of stateless services, **clear API boundaries**, and **standard** cloud patterns — we’re not reinventing infrastructure. Early stage, **scalability** is less about “infinite users tomorrow” and more about **shipping safely** and **iterating fast** without rewriting everything.

### Q: What makes your product hard to replicate?

**Honest framing:** tech alone is **rarely** uncopyable. What compounds is **workflow depth**, **partner integrations**, **trust processes**, and **data with user permission** over time — **if** we execute. Incumbents can copy **features**; **focus and ecosystem fit** in our wedge is the bet.

### Q: How do you handle security and privacy?

**Practices:** auth best practices, **least privilege**, **encryption in transit**, auditability where implemented — match your **actual** posture. For diligence, offer **summary** and **deeper doc** under NDA. Don’t claim certifications you **don’t** have.

## Risks

### Q: What are the biggest risks?

**Market:** incumbents and **slow adoption** in our wedge. **Execution:** product complexity across **two** surfaces. **Regulatory:** short-term rental rules **vary by city**. **Capital:** runway if growth is slower than planned. Naming risks **calmly** builds trust.

### Q: What could go wrong?

We could **mis-pick** the wedge, **burn** on channels that don’t convert, or **under-ship** relative to roadmap. **Mitigation:** tight **milestones**, **kill criteria** for experiments, and **capital discipline**. We **re-plan** when evidence says so.

### Q: Regulatory risks?

**Local** STR rules, **tax**, **licensing**, and **platform liability** trends differ by jurisdiction. We **monitor** priority markets, design **policy and disclosure** flows where needed, and **avoid** promising we’ve solved **every** locale — we **phase** expansion.

### Q: What keeps you up at night?

Pick **one real** issue — e.g. **density in market one**, **key hire**, **technical debt vs speed** — and what you’re **doing** about it this quarter. Authenticity beats “nothing, we’re crushing it.”

## Difficult questions (critical)

### Q: Why will you succeed where others failed?

Others failed from **wrong timing**, **unclear wedge**, or **undercapitalized** fights with incumbents. Our bet is **narrower**: **integrated ops** in **specific** segments, **capital-efficient** GTM, and **honest** iteration. **We might still fail** — but the failure mode we’re avoiding is **“boil the ocean.”**

### Q: What if Airbnb copies you?

They **can** copy **features**. They optimize for **global** consumer scale; we optimize for **our wedge** — **speed**, **partner relationships**, and **ecosystem integration** with LECIPM. If they move into our niche, **validation** went up; we **narrow** or **partner** based on facts. **No bluster** — it’s a real risk.

### Q: Why should we invest now?

**Now** is when **capital** buys **milestones that de-risk** the wedge: **product depth**, **market density**, **team**. **Later** the round may be **more expensive** or the **window** shifts. If you’re **early**, the reason is **asymmetric upside** for **first institutional** partners who **help** with network and discipline — only if that’s **true** for you.

### Q: Why haven’t you grown faster?

**Truth-based:** we **prioritized [product / compliance / foundation]**, or we **tested** and **learned X**, or **capital** was **limited**. **No excuses** — **one** lesson and **what changed**.

### Q: What happens if you don’t raise?

**Runway** and **plan B**: **leaner scope**, **revenue earlier**, **bridge**, or **strategic partner**. Be **specific** to your situation. Avoid **desperation**; show **you’ve thought it through**.

### Q: Why this valuation?

**Frame:** **comparables** (if you use them) are **directional**; **valuation** reflects **milestones ahead** and **round size**. Willing to **align** with **lead** investor on **structure**. Don’t **defend** a number you can’t support — **conversation**, not debate.
