# LECIPM Daily Founder Routine

**Practical checklist for monitoring and guiding the platform each day**

This routine helps founders keep the platform **healthy**, **safe**, and **growing** through focused daily actions. It aligns with the [Founder Control Dashboard](FOUNDER-CONTROL-DASHBOARD.md), [Founder Command Center](FOUNDER-COMMAND-CENTER.md), [Platform Operating Manual](PLATFORM-OPERATING-MANUAL.md), and [Control Blueprint](CONTROL-BLUEPRINT.md). Keep it simple and consistent; adjust timing and depth to your schedule.

---

# Section 1 — Morning Platform Check

## First checks each morning

Perform these **before** the rest of the day so you know the platform’s status and can act on issues early.

| Check | Action | Why it matters |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Review Founder Control Dashboard** | Open the [Founder Control 20](FOUNDER-CONTROL-DASHBOARD.md#the-founder-control-20-recommended-daily-set); scan all six categories. | Single view of activity, revenue, growth, risk, experience, and stability. |
| **Check booking volume** | Note bookings (yesterday / last 7 days); compare to prior period. | Core activity; a drop may indicate demand, product, or supply issue. |
| **Check new listings** | Note new listings in period; trend vs last week. | Supply health; a drop may indicate onboarding or product friction. |
| **Check revenue metrics** | GMV and platform revenue (MTD or last 7d); payment success rate. | Financial health; payment success &lt;95% needs immediate attention. |
| **Review overnight alerts** | Check alert strip or inbox for: payment failures, fraud spike, dispute backlog, uptime, critical incidents. | Catch incidents before they escalate; decide if you need to step in. |

## Why these checks matter

- **Dashboard and bookings:** Confirm the marketplace is active and on track; spot anomalies quickly.
- **Listings and revenue:** Confirm supply and monetization are healthy; revenue issues often surface overnight.
- **Alerts:** Overnight issues (payments, fraud, outages) need a same-day response; founders set the tone for urgency.

**Time:** 10–15 minutes. If any metric is red or alert is critical, extend as needed and trigger follow-up (see Section 5).

# Section 2 — Trust and Safety Review

## How to review platform safety

Founders don’t run every T&S case but should **see trends** and **know when to intervene**. Use the Trust & Safety section of the Control Dashboard and any T&S digest or escalation feed.

| Check | Action | When leadership intervention is necessary |
|-------|--------|-------------------------------------------|
| **Fraud alerts** | Note count (e.g. last 7 days); trend vs prior week. | Spike (e.g. 2× baseline); coordinated or high-value fraud; disputed payout hold. |
| **Dispute reports** | Open disputes; dispute rate; resolution time. | Backlog growing; rate above target; high-profile or precedent-setting case. |
| **Account suspensions** | Count in period; any high-impact or sensitive accounts. | Spike; public figure or partner; legal or PR risk. |
| **Listing flags** | Volume and resolution rate. | Sudden increase; unresolved backlog; policy ambiguity. |

## When to intervene

- **Routine:** T&S team handles per [Operating Manual](PLATFORM-OPERATING-MANUAL.md#section-4--trust-and-safety-operations) and [Control Blueprint](CONTROL-BLUEPRINT.md#section-7--trust-and-safety-oversight).
- **You step in when:** Material fraud or loss; regulatory or legal contact; dispute or enforcement that could set precedent or affect trust; T&S lead or CEO escalates. When in doubt, back T&S on safety; never override for short-term commercial gain.

**Time:** 5–10 minutes unless an escalation requires deeper review.

# Section 3 — Growth and Marketplace Activity

## How to monitor marketplace growth

Use the **Growth** and **Marketplace Activity** sections of the Control Dashboard. Focus on supply and activation, not only top-level GMV.

| Check | Action | Signals of healthy growth |
|-------|--------|----------------------------|
| **New hosts onboarded** | Count (e.g. last 7 days); trend. | Steady or growing; aligned with targets. |
| **New brokers onboarded** | Count and activity (e.g. first listing). | Positive trend; quality over pure volume. |
| **Referral program activity** | Referral signups and conversions to listing/booking. | Conversions and ROI in acceptable range. |
| **Listing supply growth** | Listing growth rate; active listings by market. | Positive growth; balance across markets if multi-city. |

## What to look for

- **Healthy:** Acquisition and listing growth on or above plan; activation rate stable or improving.
- **Concerning:** Sustained drop in new hosts/brokers or listing growth; activation rate down; referral ROI declining. Discuss with Growth and Ops; align on fixes (onboarding, product, incentives).

**Time:** 5 minutes unless you’re deep-diving into a specific market or channel.

# Section 4 — Product and User Experience

## How to monitor product performance

Use the **Product Experience** section of the Control Dashboard and, if available, a short support or feedback summary.

| Check | Action | How these guide product improvements |
|-------|--------|--------------------------------------|
| **Booking completion rate** | Note rate and trend. | Low rate → funnel or payment issue; prioritize with Product/Eng. |
| **Cancellation rates** | Note rate and trend. | High rate → policy, communication, or trust; refine with Product/Policy. |
| **Support ticket trends** | Volume and top categories; resolution time. | Spike → incident or UX issue; categorize and assign; resolution time up → capacity or self-serve. |
| **User feedback** | Sample of recent feedback or NPS/CSAT if available. | Recurring themes → roadmap and prioritization. |

## How to use the signals

- **Completion and cancellation:** Feed into product and ops priorities; don’t ignore sustained deterioration.
- **Support:** Ensure critical issues have owners; use trends to argue for product or process fixes.
- **Feedback:** Share with Product and leadership; use in weekly/monthly reviews.

**Time:** 5 minutes; longer if you’re reviewing a specific launch or incident.

# Section 5 — Operations Coordination

## How to coordinate with teams

Daily alignment keeps **execution** and **priorities** clear. Format can be stand-up, async update, or short call—whatever fits the team.

| Area | What to cover | Purpose of daily alignment |
|------|----------------|----------------------------|
| **Engineering** | Critical bugs, incidents, deployments; blockers. | Know stability and delivery; unblock if needed. |
| **Operations** | Marketplace ops, support load, payout and payment issues. | Ensure ops can run the platform; surface capacity or process gaps. |
| **Trust and Safety** | Alerts, escalations, backlog; any need for policy or founder input. | Support T&S; step in only when necessary. |
| **Growth** | Acquisition and activation; campaigns; partnerships. | Keep growth on track; align on priorities and constraints. |

## Purpose

- **Shared picture:** Everyone knows platform status and top issues.
- **Fast decisions:** Small decisions get made; blockers get escalated to you only when needed.
- **Culture:** Regular contact reinforces priorities (trust, safety, execution) and reduces surprises.

**Time:** 15–30 minutes total (single stand-up or separate touchpoints). Keep it crisp; defer deep dives to weekly review.

# Section 6 — Strategic Work

## Dedicating time to long-term strategy

Reserve **focused time** for work that doesn’t fit in daily ops: product strategy, partnerships, investors, expansion. If you don’t schedule it, it gets crowded out.

| Area | Examples | Why it matters for long-term success |
|------|----------|--------------------------------------|
| **Product strategy** | Roadmap, prioritization, key features or markets. | Ensures the product evolves toward [War Map](WAR-MAP.md) and [Master Strategy Book](MASTER-STRATEGY-BOOK.md) vision. |
| **Partnerships** | Outreach, negotiations, partner performance. | Accelerate supply, demand, or capability without building everything. |
| **Investor relations** | Updates, materials, board prep. | Keeps capital and governance aligned with strategy. |
| **Market expansion planning** | Next markets, compliance, resourcing. | Ensures expansion is deliberate and ready per [90-Day](90-DAY-EXECUTION-PLAN.md) and [24-Month](24-MONTH-SCALING-ROADMAP.md) plans. |

## How to protect this time

- **Block calendar:** e.g. 1–2 hours most days or 2–3 deeper blocks per week.
- **Separate from ops:** No “quick” ops in this block; use it for strategy, partnerships, and investor work.
- **Review:** In weekly/quarterly reviews, ask whether strategic work is actually happening or slipping.

**Time:** 1–2 hours per day or equivalent per week, depending on stage and team size.

# Section 7 — End-of-Day Review

## Daily closing routine

A short **end-of-day pass** helps you close the loop and set up the next day.

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **Review key metrics again** | Quick pass on Control Dashboard: bookings, revenue, payment success, alerts. Confirm nothing deteriorated since morning. |
| **Review progress on major initiatives** | Note what moved on product, growth, or expansion; what was blocked. |
| **Note operational issues that need follow-up** | List 1–3 items to follow up tomorrow (e.g. incident post-mortem, escalation, capacity decision). |
| **Prepare priorities for the next day** | Top 3 priorities for tomorrow; any prep needed (e.g. board deck, partner call). |

## Why it helps

- **Closure:** You leave with a clear picture of the day and any open loops.
- **Continuity:** Next morning you know what to prioritize without re-discovering from scratch.
- **Discipline:** Prevents “firefighting only”; keeps strategic and follow-up work visible.

**Time:** 10–15 minutes. Can be done from phone or laptop.

# Section 8 — Weekly and Monthly Extensions

## How the daily routine connects to broader reviews

The daily routine feeds **weekly** and **monthly** (and **quarterly**) reviews; it doesn’t replace them.

| Cadence | Focus | Connection to daily routine |
|---------|--------|------------------------------|
| **Weekly operational review** | Full metrics from Command Center; incidents and blockers; priorities for next week. | Use the week’s daily checks and team updates as input; resolve cross-functional issues; set next week’s focus. |
| **Monthly performance review** | GMV, revenue, supply/demand, CAC/LTV, compliance and risk summary; budget and forecast. | Aggregate daily and weekly data; compare to plan; adjust tactics or resources. |
| **Quarterly strategy review** | Progress vs roadmap and [24-Month](24-MONTH-SCALING-ROADMAP.md) plan; market expansion; product and AI roadmap; strategic bets. | Step back from daily/weekly; confirm strategy and resources; update roadmap and priorities. |

## Rhythm

- **Daily:** This routine (Control Dashboard, T&S, growth, product, ops coordination, strategic block, end-of-day review).
- **Weekly:** Operational review with leadership; use [Operating Manual](PLATFORM-OPERATING-MANUAL.md#section-13--performance-reviews) and Command Center.
- **Monthly:** Business and performance review; financial and growth metrics vs plan.
- **Quarterly:** Strategy review; roadmap and [Founder Playbook](FOUNDER-PLAYBOOK.md) alignment.

# One-Page Daily Checklist

Use this as a quick scan; expand each line using the sections above.

**Morning**
- [ ] Review Founder Control Dashboard (all six categories)
- [ ] Check booking volume and new listings
- [ ] Check revenue and payment success rate
- [ ] Review overnight alerts; act on critical items

**Trust & Safety**
- [ ] Review fraud alerts, dispute rate, suspensions, listing flags
- [ ] Note any need for leadership intervention or escalation

**Growth & Marketplace**
- [ ] Check new hosts, new brokers, referral activity, listing growth
- [ ] Note whether growth signals are healthy or concerning

**Product & UX**
- [ ] Check completion rate, cancellation rate, support volume and resolution
- [ ] Note any feedback or themes for Product

**Operations**
- [ ] Touch base with Eng, Ops, T&S, Growth (stand-up or async)
- [ ] Unblock or escalate as needed

**Strategic work**
- [ ] Dedicate blocked time to product strategy, partnerships, investors, or expansion

**End of day**
- [ ] Quick metric pass; note progress and blockers
- [ ] List 1–3 follow-ups for tomorrow
- [ ] Set top 3 priorities for next day
