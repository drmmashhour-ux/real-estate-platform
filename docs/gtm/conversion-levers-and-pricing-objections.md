# How to push conversion toward 30–50%

Most teams fail here because they skip **four controllable levers**. Nail these before you chase more traffic.

---

## The four levers

### Lever 1 — Speed

**Response time = everything**

| Behavior | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| Respond **&lt; 1 hour** | Higher conversion |
| Respond **&gt; ~12 hours** | Conversion drops hard |

**Your move**

- Push **operators** to respond **fast** to families.  
- **Remind** operators if they haven’t replied to an inbound lead.

---

### Lever 2 — Best match first

- Don’t send **10** options.  
- Send **3 max**: **1 best match** + **2 alternatives**.

---

### Lever 3 — Guide to visit

Don’t stop at “here are options.”

Always drive to:

> **“Let’s schedule a visit.”**

That step is the real conversion move.

---

### Lever 4 — Remove friction

- No **long** forms  
- No **unnecessary** complexity  
- **Fast** answers (short fields, clear next step)

---

## Target funnel (example math)

Rough planning numbers — tune to your market:

| Stage | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Users / interest | **100** |
| Qualified leads | **30** |
| Visits | **15** |
| Contracts / move-ins | **5–7** |

**Visit → contract** in the **30–50%** range is a strong **operator-side** outcome when visits are **qualified** and **follow-up is fast**.

---

## Pricing negotiation (no pushback)

### “Too expensive”

```
I completely understand — the goal isn’t cost, it’s results.

If one of these leads turns into a resident, it usually covers months of marketing.

We can also start smaller and scale only if it works for you.
```

---

### “Not sure”

```
That makes sense — that’s exactly why we start with real leads first.

You only continue if you see value; there’s no long-term commitment.
```

---

### Compares to ads

```
The difference is simple —

ads bring visibility,
we bring people ready to visit.

That’s why most partners prefer this model.
```

---

### Hesitates to pay upfront

Don’t argue — **reframe**:

```
We can keep it simple — we continue sending leads, and you pay only for qualified requests.

No fixed cost, no risk.
```

---

## Key sales rule

- **Never** defend the price alone  
- **Always** reframe **value** (outcome per dollar)

---

## What you’re really selling

**Not:** ❌ “leads” as an abstract commodity  

**But:** ✅ **residents** · **occupancy** · **revenue**
