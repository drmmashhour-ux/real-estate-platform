# Outreach strategy

## Channels

- **Email** — Primary for clarity and attachments (deck link when appropriate).
- **LinkedIn** — Connection requests + short DMs; use for people who are active there or when email is unknown.

Do not duplicate the same person on both channels in the same week unless one channel failed silently.

---

## Volume (guideline)

- **Daily:** ~**10–20** qualified outreach actions (first touches + scheduled follow-ups combined — adjust to your bandwidth).
- Quality beats quantity: better to send fewer well-researched notes than many generic ones.

---

## Sequence (single thread)

| Step | Timing | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Initial message — personalized + clear ask. |
| 2 | +3–5 days | Follow-up 1 — brief reminder + value (e.g. demo link, one-line update). |
| 3 | +7–10 days from first | Follow-up 2 — very short. |
| 4 | +14–21 days from first | Final follow-up — close loop, leave door open. |

**Stop** if they reply (even “not now”) — move to **response-handling.md**.

---

## Message rules

- **Short** — no long paragraphs; one screen on mobile.
- **Personalized first line** — see § Personalization below.
- **Clear ask** — typically **15–20 minute** intro call; avoid vague “pick your brain.”
- **Factual only** — describe what the product *is* and *does*; do not invent users, revenue, or growth metrics.
- **One primary CTA** per message.

---

## Links (link management)

Standardize a small set of URLs (use your real production or staging URLs):

| Asset | When to send |
|-------|----------------|
| **Landing / marketing home** | First or second touch — one link. |
| **Demo** (product tour or demo hub) | Follow-up 1 or when they ask for a look. |
| **Deck** | When they ask, or after expressed interest — not three links in the first cold email. |

**Rules**

- First cold message: **at most one** link (usually landing or calendar).
- Keep URLs clean (no long UTM chains in the first message unless you track them elsewhere).
- Verify links before each send.

---

## Personalization system (before every send)

Before sending any message:

1. **One sentence** specific to them — e.g. their fund, a portfolio company they’ve backed, or a stated focus area.
2. **Max one sentence** — do not stack multiple compliments.
3. **Do not overdo it** — if you cannot find anything real, use a neutral professional opener instead of fake familiarity.

**Examples (factual patterns only)**

- “I saw [Fund] lists B2B SaaS in your thesis — we’re building workflow software for real estate brokerages.”
- “I noticed you invested in [Company] — adjacent problem space for us.”

**If you cannot verify the fact, do not claim it.**

---

## Iteration

- **Weekly:** review which angles and subject lines get replies (see **messaging-angles.md**).
- Adjust templates; do not change more than one variable at a time if you want clean learning.
