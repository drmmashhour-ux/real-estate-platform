# Response handling

Keep replies **short**, **professional**, and **appreciative**. Never argue with a pass.

---

## 1. Investor is interested

**Goal:** Book a time quickly; reduce back-and-forth.

**Template:**

Thanks [Name] — really appreciate the quick reply. I’m free [two options with time zones] for **20 minutes**. If easier, here’s my calendar: [link].

Happy to send a one-pager or deck ahead if useful.

[Your name]

---

## 2. Investor asks for more info

**Goal:** Send **one** focused asset (deck or short memo) + offer a call.

**Template:**

Thanks — attaching / linking [deck / memo]. Happy to walk through it on a **15-minute** call; I can also answer specific questions by email if you prefer.

[Your name]  
[Single link]

---

## 3. Investor declines (not a fit / timing / thesis)

**Goal:** Thank them; leave relationship intact.

**Template:**

Thanks for the clear note — totally understand. If anything shifts on your side, I’m glad to reconnect. Wishing you and [Firm] well.

[Your name]

**Do not** push for a call after a firm no.

---

## 4. No response (after full sequence)

**Goal:** Already handled by **final follow-up** in email-templates.md. If they reply months later, treat as a fresh thread — polite, no guilt.

**Template:**

Thanks for getting back — happy to find time for a **short call** this month. What works on your side?

---

## 5. “Send deck only” / async only

**Template:**

Understood — here’s [link]. If anything stands out, I’m happy to answer questions async or jump on a **short call**.

---

## Logging

Update your tracker: **Status** = `Replied` / `Meeting` / `Passed` as appropriate; add **Notes** with date and summary.
