# Law 25 (Québec) — privacy operations checklist

**Purpose:** **Operational** readiness aligned with common **Law 25** themes.  
**Not exhaustive.** **Not legal advice.** Québec’s *Act respecting the protection of personal information in the private sector* (as modernized) has **specific requirements**—this list is a **starting point** for discussion with counsel and your **person in charge of personal information**.

**Official guidance:** Consult the **Commission d’accès à l’information du Québec (CAI)** and qualified privacy counsel for authoritative interpretation.

---

## 1. Governance

- [ ] **Person responsible:** Designate who is **accountable** for personal information (title and backup).
- [ ] **Policies:** Privacy policy and internal policies **documented** and **reviewed** on a schedule.
- [ ] **Roles:** Map who handles HR data, customer data, logs, and vendor relationships.

## 2. Processes

- [ ] **Access requests:** Internal steps to **locate** and **respond** to access requests within **legal timelines** (verify current rules).
- [ ] **Correction / deletion:** Process for updates and **challenges** to accuracy.
- [ ] **Consent:** Where consent is required, **record** how it was collected and allow **withdrawal** where applicable.
- [ ] **Retention:** **Table** of categories → **retention period** → **deletion** method (align with Privacy Policy after counsel review).

## 3. Security

- [ ] **Safeguards** appropriate to sensitivity (access control, MFA for admin, encryption in transit, secrets management).
- [ ] **Vendor review:** DPAs / processing terms with **major** subprocessors (hosting, email, payments, analytics).
- [ ] **Incident response:** **Runbook**: detect → contain → assess → **notify** (internal + **regulator/individuals** as **required by law**) → document.

## 4. Transparency

- [ ] **Website / app:** Privacy Policy and (where applicable) **cookie** notice **accessible** before or at collection.
- [ ] **New features:** **Privacy review** before shipping AI, analytics, or **new** data collection (especially sensitive categories if any).

## 5. Records and training

- [ ] **Training:** Staff with data access receive **periodic** privacy/security awareness.
- [ ] **Logs of incidents** (even near-misses) for **improvement**—retention per counsel advice.

## 6. Cross-border transfers

- [ ] If data leaves Québec/Canada, document **mechanism** (adequacy, contractual clauses, etc.)—**counsel required**.

## 7. Bilingual / public-facing

- [ ] If you **substantially** target Québec consumers, discuss **French** versions of key policies and **Bill 96** publicity rules with counsel—not covered here.

**Update:** Revisit after regulatory guidance or **material** product change.
