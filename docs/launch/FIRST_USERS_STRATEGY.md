# First users strategy

**Goal:** **Conversations**, not mass signups. Optimize for depth with the right people.

---

## Who to target first

| Segment | Why |
|---------|-----|
| **Brokers** (individual) | Decision-maker is the user; fast feedback loop. |
| **Small agencies** | More volume if one champion adopts; still manageable for support. |
| **People you already know** | Highest trust, honest feedback, referrals. |

Defer: large brokerages with IT/security gates until you have proof points and bandwidth.

---

## Where to find them

| Channel | How to use it |
|---------|----------------|
| **Personal network** | “I’m building X for Y—can I show you 15 minutes?” |
| **LinkedIn** | Thoughtful DMs to second-degree connections; comment on their posts first when natural. |
| **University / alumni** | Clubs, real estate programs, founder networks. |
| **Local real estate community** | Meetups, associations, masterminds—one genuine relationship beats 100 cold emails. |

Avoid buying lists or spammy automation in the first phase.

---

## How to reach out

1. **Direct message** — short, specific, about *their* pain, not your roadmap essay.  
2. **Personal intro** — ask a mutual contact: “Would you intro me to [name] for feedback?”  
3. **Demo invitation** — “I’d love to show you how we handle [X]. 20 minutes this week?”

**Template structure (adapt every time)**

- One line context (who you are).  
- One line **their** problem space.  
- One ask: **short call** or **async Loom** if they prefer.  
- Optional: social proof only if true (“3 brokers testing with us”).

---

## What “good” looks like

- **Reply rate** improves as targeting improves (track loosely).  
- Demos **booked**, not just “sounds interesting.”  
- You can name **5 recurring objections**—then you fix product or messaging.

---

## What to avoid

- Pitching **every** feature—leads to confusion.  
- Arguing on calls; **listen**, then show the smallest demo that fits.  
- Scaling spend before **repeat usage** from early users—see [LAUNCH_PLAN](./LAUNCH_PLAN.md).
