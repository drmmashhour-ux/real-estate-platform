# Launch execution plan

**Objective:** Land **10–50 real users** (people who log in, run workflows, and give honest feedback)—not vanity signups.

**Primary focus**

- Individual **brokers** and associate agents  
- **Small agencies** (low headcount, fast decisions)  
- **Personal network** first (warm intros beat cold lists)

**Principles**

1. **Soft launch → controlled users.** Invite people you can support personally; widen only when support and product stability allow it.  
2. **Demo-first.** Let prospects *see* the product before asking for commitment.  
3. **Feedback-driven iteration.** Ship small fixes weekly; avoid a long silent build cycle.  
4. **No mass marketing initially.** No paid blasts, no spray-and-pray—until messaging and onboarding survive real conversations.

**Core rule**

> **Get real usage before scaling.**  
> Prove repeat visits, a clear “aha” moment, and tolerable support load *before* investing in broad acquisition or heavy infra.

## User onboarding strategy

**Goal:** User reaches an **“aha moment”** fast (e.g. “I see how this tracks my client / deal / doc in one place”) with **minimal friction**.

| Layer | What to do |
|-------|------------|
| **Guided demo** | Walk them live ([DEMO_PROCESS](./DEMO_PROCESS.md)); optionally reuse in-app demo mode if you have it. |
| **First session after signup** | One simple path: **add a client** *or* **create/open a deal**—pick the single path that matches your strongest flow today. |
| **Forms** | Short fields only; defer “nice to have” profile data. |
| **No long onboarding wizards** in week 1–4 unless required for compliance; add depth after repeat usage. |

If they stall, **book a 15-minute setup call**—cheaper than losing a warm user.

## Outreach execution (daily / weekly)

**Daily (weekdays)**

- Send **10–20** personalized messages (DMs or emails)—quality over volume.  
- **Follow up** with anyone who replied but didn’t book (one polite nudge, then park).  
- **Book demos** while energy is high; aim for concrete times, not “let’s circle back.”

**Weekly**

- Review **which messages got replies**; copy what worked, drop what didn’t.  
- Adjust **one variable** at a time (subject line, opener, CTA to book demo).  
- Keep cadence **consistent**—momentum beats occasional hero weeks.

## Trust building

- **Transparency:** Say it’s early; you’re building with users, not hiding behind “enterprise-ready” fluff.  
- **No overselling:** Promise only what works today; note what’s on the roadmap explicitly.  
- **Value first:** Tie every screen to a broker pain (follow-ups, docs, offers, chaos reduction).  
- **Professional follow-up:** Thank-you after demos, clear next step, show you remember their context.  
- **Testimonials:** Ask only when someone is **genuinely** happy; a short LinkedIn post or quote beats fake case studies.

## Risks during launch (and mitigations)

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| **Demo bugs** | Run the demo path **same day** before calls; keep a fallback (screenshots / Loom). Reset demo data per [DEMO_PROCESS](./DEMO_PROCESS.md). |
| **Unclear UX** | Capture confusion in [FEEDBACK_LOOP](./FEEDBACK_LOOP.md); fix copy and empty states before new features. |
| **Users don’t get the value** | Reframe demo around **one job** they care about; ask “what would make you try this for real?” in Q&A. |
| **Low response rate** | Improve targeting (warmer intros); shorten messages; ask mutual contacts for introductions; accept that cold outreach has a low hit rate—optimize for learning. |
| **Support overwhelm** | Cap active invites; batch office hours; document top 5 FAQs. |

---

## Success (first phase)

You’re on track when:

- Demos happen **regularly** and finish with a clear next step.  
- A handful of users **return** without you chasing.  
- Feedback produces **visible** changes within a week or two.  
- You can describe **who** the product is for and **what** job it does in one sentence—validated by users, not only by you.
