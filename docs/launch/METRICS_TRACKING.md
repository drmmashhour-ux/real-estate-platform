# Metrics tracking (early stage)

**Goal:** Enough signal to see **engagement** and **progress**—not a full analytics program.

**Rule:** Do **not** overcomplicate. A spreadsheet or admin counts you can export weekly is enough at first.

---

## What to track (essential)

| Metric | Why | How (examples) |
|--------|-----|----------------|
| **Demos completed** | Funnel top | Calendar count or simple log |
| **Users onboarded** | Accounts you care about | Manual list or DB export |
| **Active users (rough)** | Are they coming back? | Weekly “logged in” or “meaningful action”—define one bar and keep it consistent |
| **Deals created** (or equivalent core object) | Core workflow adoption | Count per week |
| **Documents uploaded** | File workflow traction | Count per week |
| **Messages sent** | Collaboration signal | Count per week |

Pick **one** primary “north star” object if your product centers on deals vs listings vs clients—don’t dilute with ten KPIs.

---

## What not to track (yet)

- Funnel with 12 steps, cohort retention models, attribution across channels—**later**, when volume justifies it.  
- Vanity metrics (page views without action) unless you’re debugging marketing.

---

## Cadence

- **Weekly:** Update the sheet; note **anomalies** (spike, drop, one power user skewing).  
- **Monthly:** Step back—trend vs last month, not daily noise.

---

## Using the numbers

- **Flat activity** after onboarding → onboarding or value problem; go to users ([FEEDBACK_LOOP](./FEEDBACK_LOOP.md)).  
- **Growing docs/messages** with stagnant deals → users using a slice of product; align demo and first-run to full workflow.  
- **Demos up, onboarded flat** → friction in signup or unclear next step—fix before more outreach ([LAUNCH_PLAN](./LAUNCH_PLAN.md)).

---

## Honesty

If counts are **manual**, say so. Rough numbers that drive decisions beat perfect dashboards nobody looks at.
