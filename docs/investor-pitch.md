## Pitch narrative (arc)

1. **Hook** — Real estate and stays are fragmented; trust and operations don’t scale on disconnected tools.  
2. **Problem** — Professionals, hosts, and guests juggle systems; verification and transparency are inconsistent.  
3. **Solution** — One **integrated** platform: LECIPM (real-estate ecosystem) + **BNHub** (short-term rentals) sharing identity, data, and governance where architected.  
4. **Product** — What exists today vs roadmap (be explicit).  
5. **Market** — Large categories; we win **density** in chosen geographies first — not “global day one.”  
6. **Business model** — Take rate, subscriptions, featured supply — see [financials.md](financials.md).  
7. **Traction** — **Only real numbers**; if early, show pilots, waitlists, LOIs, product milestones, and experiments ([metrics](#metrics-realistic) below).  
8. **Growth** — GTM from [enterprise-sales.md](enterprise-sales.md), city playbooks, content — no guaranteed CAC.  
9. **Competition** — OTAs and portals are strong; we differentiate on **ecosystem integration**, **trust workflows**, and **AI-assisted ops** where shipped — not on claiming monopoly.  
10. **Tech** — Modular monorepo, APIs, web (mobile as roadmap or beta — state truthfully).  
11. **Team** — Why this team; gaps you will hire for with the round.  
12. **Vision** — Trusted operating layer for property + stays in priority markets.  
13. **Ask** — Round size, use of funds, milestones for next 12–18 months.

## Script (spoken) — 5–7 minutes

**Tone:** Simple, confident, precise. Pause after problem and ask.

---

**[Hook — 20s]**  
“I’m \[name\]. We’re building BNHub inside LECIPM — one platform where short-term rentals and real-estate workflows connect, instead of living in separate tools that don’t share trust or data.”

**[Problem — 45s]**  
“Today, property managers, brokers, and hosts use a patchwork of listing sites, spreadsheets, and messaging. Guests don’t always see full price or verification upfront. Operators can’t scale support and compliance without throwing people at the problem. The result is friction, mistrust, and lost bookings — not because people don’t want to transact, but because the stack is fragmented.”

**[Solution — 40s]**  
“We’re integrating discovery, booking, and professional tooling in one ecosystem: LECIPM for the broader real-estate surface, BNHub for stays — with shared verification, payments narrative, and admin operations. The point isn’t feature parity with every OTA on day one; it’s **trust + integration** so serious hosts and partners can run volume without chaos.”

**[Product — 90s]**  
“On BNHub, we show the guest path: search, listing detail with clear fees where implemented, booking flow. On the host side: listings, calendar, pricing — what’s live in your build, say so. On LECIPM: how professionals and owners attach to the same identity and data layer. If something is pilot-only, name it as pilot. Investors punish ‘vapor’; they reward honesty with clear milestones.”

**[Market — 35s]**  
“Short-term rentals and adjacent real-estate software are large, mature categories. We’re not claiming we out-SEO global incumbents tomorrow. We’re starting with **geographic depth** — prove liquidity and repeat behavior — then expand. Our market slide uses **directional** TAM; our plan uses **bottom-up** city math.”

**[Business model — 35s]**  
“Booking commissions, premium host tools, and featured placement where ROI-positive — detailed in our financial appendix. All forward numbers are **estimates**, not GAAP forecasts.”

**[Traction — 40s]**  
“Here’s what is **true today**: \[users, listings, bookings, pilots, revenue if any\]. Here’s what we’re **proving next**: \[specific milestone in next 90 days\]. We do not smooth curves or rename pilots as ‘traction’ — we label stage clearly.”

**[Competition & moat — 35s]**  
“Airbnb and others own consumer habit at scale. We respect that. We compete where **integration with real-estate operations** and **transparent trust workflows** matter — property managers, agencies, multi-unit hosts — and we improve with data and AI **where we’ve actually shipped** it.”

**[Growth / GTM — 35s]**  
“City launch playbooks, B2B pipeline, content, and partnerships — see our GTM slide. Everything is measured; we kill what doesn’t move pipeline or bookings.”

**[Team — 25s]**  
“Why us, who we need to hire with this round — product, GTM, trust ops.”

**[Vision — 20s]**  
“A trusted operating layer for stays and property in our first markets — then broader Canada and international with the same discipline.”

**[Ask — 30s]**  
“We’re raising \[amount\] to \[product / growth / ops split from deck\]. We’re looking for partners who care about **execution under uncertainty** — we’ll report honestly and hit milestones we can control.”

**[Close]**  
“Happy to show the product live and walk through assumptions. What questions do you have?”

## Metrics (realistic)

**Replace placeholders with your actuals before sending materials.**

| Metric | How to present |
|--------|----------------|
| Users | Registered or MAU — define the term; no blending |
| Listings | Published vs draft; per market if relevant |
| Bookings | Count + GMV if you disclose; time window stated |
| Conversion | Funnel stage definitions consistent with internal dashboards |
| Early stage | Experiments: “X outreach, Y replies, Z demos” — honest counts |

If numbers are small, **lead with learning and speed of iteration**, not vanity multiples.

## Outreach strategy

1. **Tier investors** — thesis fit (marketplace, proptech, Canada), cheque size, portfolio conflicts.  
2. **Warm intro first** — founder network, angels, operators.  
3. **Materials** — one-pager + deck link (password optional); data room later.  
4. **Process** — intro call → partner meeting → diligence → terms.  
5. **Follow-up** — see [investor-outreach.md](investor-outreach.md).

## Optional demo flow (live)

1. **Guest:** City or search → listing → price clarity → booking step (sandbox payment if needed).  
2. **Host:** Dashboard → listing edit or calendar (what’s stable).  
3. **Ops:** Admin growth or trust queue **if** appropriate for audience — no internal-only bugs on first call.

Rehearse with **staging**; avoid production data leaks.
