# Early revenue playbook — LECIPM

## When to offer first listing free

- Cold outbound or inbound with **low trust**.
- You need a **reference process** (“here’s how we work”) more than immediate revenue.
- Competitive listings where the owner is **already paying** elsewhere — reduce friction to try you once.

## When to switch to paid listing ($15)

- After they’ve seen **one cycle** of promotion or a clear deliverable.
- When they add **additional** properties or want ongoing placement.
- When operational cost (time, boosts, creative) needs to be covered.

## When to charge per qualified lead ($275)

- They want **ongoing** introductions, not a one-off post.
- You’ve **defined “qualified”** in writing (budget band, move-in window, viewing commitment).
- Volume is manageable and you can **dispute-proof** the handoff (timestamp, channel export).

## How to explain pricing

- “We have a simple menu: first listing on us to prove fit, then either a small listing fee or per qualified lead — whichever matches how you want to work.”
- Tie price to **work and risk**, not magic outcomes.

## How to handle objections

Listen first; respond with one clarifying question, then one concrete next step.

## How to keep the first user after initial success

- **Set expectations** on response time and what you need from them (photos, availability).
- **One recap** after the first week (“here’s what we did / what we saw”).
- Offer the **next step** that matches their scale (second property, per-lead, or pause).

## Five common objections — suggested responses

**1. “I already get too many messages.”**  
“We’re not trying to add noise — we focus on getting your listing in front of people who are more likely to match what you’re offering. If volume stays high but quality is still off, we adjust targeting and messaging together.”

**2. “Can you guarantee tenants / a sale?”**  
“No one can ethically guarantee that. What we can do is improve exposure and pre-qualify so you spend time on better conversations.”

**3. “Why not just use Marketplace?”**  
“Marketplace works for reach; many owners still struggle with low-intent inquiries. We layer promotion and qualification so your time isn’t the bottleneck.”

**4. “$275 per lead sounds expensive.”**  
“Fair — it’s for leads that meet the definition we agree on. If you prefer, we can use the smaller listing option or start with the first listing free so you can judge fit.”

**5. “I don’t want AI touching my listing.”**  
“We use tools to help with promotion and drafting — you approve what goes live. Nothing replaces your facts and your approval.”
